Disconnection with gsd

Remote GSD

While public space means that everyone has the right to voice out in the shared room, sometimes students at GSD don't have enough access to the information from GSD administration under the pandemic.

The problem is that although students’ opinions have been expressed on the digital platform, these voices were not delivered to the administration. Furthermore, the administration could ignore the whoop or make quite a less effective response. The recent changes of MDes program is a good illustration for this very disconnection between students and the adminstration

Historical protest at harvard

Red Fist

Historically, students also argued with the administration for their rights via making posters. In 1969, Graduate School of Design students established the Strike Poster Workshop in the Great Space of Robinson Hall.

They chose the red fist as the symbol of their dissent, designed silkscreens and cut stencils, and printed the fist on paper, t-shirts, and armbands. The workshop created a variety of strike poster designs with an assortment of protest slogans.

This is a new digital platform to customize posters as new means of communication at the Harvard GSD virtual community. To make visible hidden reality to the public, it extracts underlying rules and design languages from significant posters design at GSD before the pandemic. These rules and conventions are translated into visual patterns in a system for potential customization.

You can choose your own template and customzie your text along with options of different graphic patterns. You can also identify yourslef with different colors set up for different programs at GSD
